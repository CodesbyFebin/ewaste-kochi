NIST 800-88 Explained — Data Sanitization Guide

NIST Special Publication 800-88 "Guidelines for Media Sanitization" is the global standard for securely wiping data from storage devices. Here's what it means for your Kochi business.

📦 Walk-In Drop-Off Available at Our Thrippunithura Facility

NIST 800-88 is published by the US National Institute of Standards and Technology. It defines three categories of media sanitization: Clear (software overwrite), Purge (degaussing or cryptographic erasure), and Destroy (physical shredding). EWaste Kochi implements all three methods depending on your security requirements.

Clear — Software Overwrite

Overwrites all addressable storage locations with non-sensitive data. Protects against simple data recovery tools. Best for drives that will be reused.

Purge — Degaussing / Cryptographic Erasure

Applies more invasive sanitization than Clear. Protects against laboratory attacks. NSA-approved degaussing permanently renders magnetic drives unreadable.

Destroy — Physical Destruction

Industrial shredding or disintegration of physical media. Highest level of security. Required for top-secret classified data. EWaste Kochi provides witnessed on-site shredding.

DoD 5220.22-M Explained

The US Department of Defense data sanitization standard. 7-pass overwrite protocol. Learn how we apply DoD 5220.22-M at EWaste Kochi for guaranteed data destruction.

DoD 5220.22-M specifies a 7-pass overwrite sequence: passes 1, 3, 5, 7 write random characters; pass 2 writes the complement of pass 1; passes 4, 6 write specific bit patterns. Each pass is verified. The result is data that cannot be recovered even with electron microscopy. EWaste Kochi applies this standard to all enterprise ITAD engagements.

For classified or high-security data (financial, legal, government), we perform physical shredding to ≤1mm particles — the only method that guarantees NIST 800-88 Destroy-level outcomes for NAND flash regardless of drive firmware state. What is the difference between degaussing and physical shredding?

Degaussing uses a powerful electromagnetic field to permanently erase all magnetic domains on spinning platters (HDDs and magnetic tapes). It is extremely effective but has two limitations: it does not work on SSDs or flash memory (which are non-magnetic), and the drive becomes physically inoperable after degaussing. Physical shredding mechanically reduces the storage media — whether HDD, SSD, USB drive, or optical disk — to particles ≤2mm, rendering data unrecoverable by any technology including electron microscopy. For maximum assurance, we recommend degaussing followed by shredding for HDDs, and shredding-only for SSDs, which maps to NIST 800-88 Destroy level for both media types.
