IT Asset Disposition (ITAD) Services in Kochi — What We Do

IT Asset Disposition is not simply throwing old computers away. It is a documented, auditable process that covers the entire end-of-life journey of your IT equipment: from secure collection through certified data destruction to environmentally compliant disposal or value recovery through refurbishment and resale.

Corporate ITAD for Kochi Businesses

Our corporate ITAD service is designed for organisations of any size — from Kakkanad startups with 10 devices to Infopark MNCs refreshing 500-unit laptop fleets. The process includes:

  • On-site inventory: Every device tagged by serial number before leaving your premises, with a chain-of-custody manifest signed by your representative and our collection team.
  • Secure transport: Devices transported in locked, tamper-evident containers to our Thrippunithura processing facility.
  • NIST 800-88 data destruction: Every storage device receives certified data wiping at Purge level (or Destroy for devices requiring physical destruction). Our wipe process generates an automated per-device log.
  • Certificate of Destruction: A legally defensible document per device, specifying the serial number, destruction method, NIST 800-88 level, and KSPCB authorization reference.
  • Asset recovery: Devices with residual market value — premium laptops, recent smartphones, lightly used servers — are valued transparently with proceeds paid to your organisation.
  • Compliance documentation package: A consolidated PDF audit report covering all devices, destruction methods, and compliance references — ready for DPDP Act 2023, RBI IT governance, NABH, or client audit purposes.

NIST 800-88 Data Destruction

NIST SP 800-88 — published by the US National Institute of Standards and Technology — is the global gold standard for media sanitisation. It defines three levels of data destruction that EWasteKochi implements as follows:

  • Clear: Overwriting using standard read/write commands. EWasteKochi implements Clear for devices being reused internally within client organisations.
  • Purge: Firmware-level sanitisation using manufacturer-specific commands (ATA Secure Erase, Cryptographic Erase for SSDs). Renders data irrecoverable by all known laboratory methods. EWasteKochi implements Purge as the minimum standard for all corporate client devices leaving organisational control.
  • Destroy: Physical destruction rendering the media permanently inoperable — shredding to 2mm particle size. EWasteKochi implements Destroy for devices containing the most sensitive data categories, high-security government clients, banking environments, and wherever a witnessed physical destruction event is required.

⚠️ DPDP Act 2023 Warning: Disposing of IT assets without a Certificate of Destruction that documents certified data destruction can expose your organisation to Data Protection Board penalties up to ₹250 Crore under India's Digital Personal Data Protection Act 2023. Contact us to understand your specific compliance obligations before disposing of any equipment containing customer or employee personal data.

Why Data Destruction Matters Before Contacting Any Recycler

Many organisations in Kochi underestimate the data risk present on decommissioned IT equipment. A formatted hard drive is not a wiped hard drive. A factory reset phone is not a data-destroyed phone. Free recovery tools available to anyone — Recuva, TestDisk, PhotoRec — can recover files from a formatted drive in under ten minutes.

The risks extend beyond competitive intelligence. Under DPDP Act 2023, health data (found on hospital workstations and tablets), financial data (found on bank branch computers and ATM infrastructure), and employee personal data (found on virtually every corporate device) are all classified as sensitive personal data requiring heightened protection. Their presence on a device that was inadequately sanitised and disposed of through a non-authorised channel creates direct organisational liability.

Who Is at Risk?

Any Kochi business that has ever handled customer, patient, employee, or vendor personal data on any computing device has DPDP Act 2023 obligations when disposing of that device. This includes:

  • IT and ITES companies at Infopark and Smart City Kochi processing client data
  • Banks and NBFCs disposing of branch workstations, ATM controllers, or server infrastructure
  • Private hospitals disposing of HMIS workstations, radiology systems, and administrative computers
  • Educational institutions disposing of student-facing devices from government laptop schemes
  • Startups and SMEs across Ernakulam district refreshing employee devices
  • Government departments and PSUs disposing of end-of-life computing infrastructure

Why KSPCB Authorization Matters When Choosing a Recycler

The Kerala State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB) issues authorization to e-waste recyclers, dismantlers, and refurbishers under the E-Waste Management Rules 2022. This authorization is not a voluntary quality signal — it is a legal prerequisite for processing electronic waste in Kerala.

More critically for businesses: E-Waste Management Rules 2022 impose obligations on bulk consumers — organisations generating e-waste above specified thresholds — to channel their e-waste exclusively through KSPCB-authorized entities. Handing devices to a roadside scrap dealer, an unauthorized recycler, or even a well-meaning but unlicensed organisation constitutes a violation by your company, regardless of the recycler's intentions.

EWasteKochi holds valid, current KSPCB authorization. We provide a copy of our authorization certificate to every corporate client for inclusion in their procurement and compliance records. Using EWasteKochi automatically satisfies the KSPCB compliance requirement embedded in your organization's e-waste obligations.
