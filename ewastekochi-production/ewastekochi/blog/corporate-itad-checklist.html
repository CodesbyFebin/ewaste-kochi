Corporate ITAD Checklist: 12 Steps Before Disposing IT Assets

✍️ EWasteKochi Editorial Team 📅 March 23, 2026 ⏱ 8 min read

Most companies think ITAD (IT Asset Disposition) is simply 'getting rid of old computers.' It isn't. Done correctly, ITAD protects your company from data breach liability, ensures DPDP Act compliance, maximises asset recovery value, and creates an auditable paper trail. Done incorrectly, it can cost you far more than the assets were worth.

Why This Matters for Kochi Businesses in 2026

The regulatory landscape for IT asset disposal has changed dramatically. With the DPDP Act 2023 now enforceable and E-Waste Management Rules 2022 in full effect, Kochi businesses face real legal consequences for improper disposal — not just reputational risk, but documented financial penalties.

Understanding this topic isn't optional for your IT or compliance team. It's foundational to operating safely in 2026.

The Complete Picture

There are several layers to this issue that most businesses — even well-resourced ones — typically miss until they're dealing with the consequences. We'll walk through each one systematically, with specific implications for businesses operating in Kochi, Ernakulam, and Kerala more broadly.

This guide is written for IT managers, CIOs, operations heads, and business owners who need to make informed decisions about their IT asset disposition process — without needing a law degree or data science background to understand the risks.

What the Regulations Actually Require

The E-Waste Management Rules 2022 are explicit: bulk consumers — defined as businesses generating e-waste — must hand over all e-waste exclusively to KSPCB-authorized recyclers. Using an unauthorized vendor, even if they offer a higher price or faster pickup, constitutes a violation.

The DPDP Act 2023 adds a data protection layer. Any personal data stored on disposed devices that becomes accessible — through a data breach or forensic recovery — triggers liability for the original data fiduciary. That means your company, not the recycler, bears responsibility for data that walks out the door on an improperly wiped hard drive.

Practical Steps for Compliance

  • Verify your recycler holds a valid KSPCB authorization certificate before any pickup
  • Request NIST 800-88 Purge or Destroy level data sanitization — not just a factory reset
  • Demand a Certificate of Destruction with serial number tracking for every device
  • Maintain chain-of-custody documentation from device collection through final destruction
  • Store CoDs for a minimum of 3 years for DPDP Act audit purposes

Frequently Asked Questions

Is a factory reset enough to delete all data?

No. Factory resets remove the file index but leave data physically on the storage medium. NIST 800-88 Purge (overwrite) or Destroy (physical shredding) is required for certified deletion that cannot be reversed by any known recovery method.

How do I get a Certificate of Destruction in Kochi?

Use a KSPCB-authorized recycler like EWasteKochi. We issue a Certificate of Destruction with individual device serial numbers for every job. You can verify any certificate online here.

What is the penalty for non-compliance under DPDP Act 2023?

Penalties under DPDP Act 2023 can reach up to ₹250 Crore for significant data breaches resulting from negligent data processing or disposal practices. The Act also allows the Data Protection Board to mandate corrective action and public disclosure.
