E-Waste Compliance: Kerala 2026

KSPCB Authorized • Form 2 Documentation • EPR Compliance

Regulatory Framework for Ernakulam Corporations

As Kochi centralizes its position as a global IT destination via SmartCity and Infopark Phase 2, the Kerala State Pollution Control Board (KSPCB) has operationalized stringent monitoring for E-Waste Management. In 2026, compliance is no longer optional; it is a core corporate governance requirement.

Under the E-Waste Management Rules, every business entity is classified as a 'Bulk Consumer' and must maintain 'Form 2' records of all decommissioned electronic inventory. Authorized disposal involves specialized dismantling where hazardous components like lead-acid batteries and mercury-containing LCDs are stabilized before recycling.

Certified Data Sanitization

NIST 800-88 • DPDP Act 2023 • Forensic Data Wipe

The NIST 800-88 Standard in India

For financial institutions and legal firms in Kerala, data integrity is the highest priority. Standard formatting is insufficient for modern NVMe and SSD storage architecture. EWasteKochi implements the NIST 800-88 Revision 1 guidelines to ensure every bit of data is forensically unrecoverable.

The Digital Personal Data Protection (DPDP) Act 2023 mandates secure erasure upon the fulfillment of data purpose. Our certified wiping process provides the forensic audit trail necessary to mitigate against the ₹250 crore liability risk associated with data breaches from retired IT assets.
